Redesigning a local frozen yogurt store's website


Introducing Berryline

BerryLine is a local frozen yogurt and ice cream shop in Cambridge, MA. They have two locations, one in Harvard Square and one in Porter Square. Their website is a place for customers to find location hours and addresses, the products they offer, job opportunities, and a variety of other information. They briefly describe the flavors they have on sale and the other types of desserts and drinks that they serve.


Identifying Problems

At first glance, the BerryLine website appears to be clean and simple. However, upon further exploration, it becomes apparent that there is a ton of information, and that much of it is not easily accessible. The design flaws become even more apparent when evaluating the website in terms of the government’s usability criteria.
Intuitive Design
The navigation bar is usable, but the page names are not very helpful in indicating the information that can be found on each page. For example, the “HOME” page contains a plethora of information, including current flavors, an “About Us” section, a menu and more. The overwhelming amount of information on a single page makes it difficult for users to find what they need. The location pages display the location, address and hours. The map is located two sections after the address is actually listed. It’s position so far down makes it not immediately helpful to the user. The color blocking on the page gives the sense that the webpage ends earlier than it does. Additionally, each location page contains a history of the location. However, it hidden at the bottom of the page, after a “Contact” section, which usually is placed at the bottom of webpages. Also, the visibility of system status isn’t great. The user has no idea that the flavors of the yogurt change on a weekly basis and when they update.
Ease of Learning
The website is more difficult to learn that it should be. As an example, a new user would likely have trouble locating a menu as there is no menu tab. The option to buy a gift card is not very easy to find as it is at the very bottom of the “HOME” tab. A new user also might want to learn more about the product from a nutritional perspective. Is it vegan? Are there dairy-free options? However, the yogurt information page is hidden in a link labeled “More Information” under the yogurt item on the menu on the “HOME” tab.
Efficiency of Use and Memorability
The website’s small size makes it easy to navigate the pages quickly. However, the confusing layout and the links scattered around the website make it difficult for people to navigate and find what is needed, leading to needless searching around and decreasing memorability. Additionally, there’s too much white space. It makes everything very far apart and not efficient to use.The website is memorable to a certain extent, but the lack of intuitive design hinders that process. It is difficult to use recognition rather than recall on this website.

Wireframing

After finding so many issues, we wanted to redesign the BerryLine website to improve its usability.




We started by creating wireframes for the “Home” page, the “Locations” page, the “Menu” page, the “Contact” page, and the “Our Story” page. Note that one of the most significant changes we made was in redesigning the pages found on the navigation bar.


Visual Redesign

Next, we took two of our wireframes and made them into high-fidelity interfaces to demonstrate what the website should look like in production.
On the left in the home page, colors from the logo are used as accent colors throughout the page to keep the color palette consistent. They also provide contrast against the white background.

Lettering on the images shows the user exactly what flavor they are looking at. We have added a translucent box on top of each image to make the text is readable, varying opacity to account for the colour under it.

Using uniform pictures instead of simply displaying the flavor names provides continuity on the page. Further, the textures of the close up ice cream shots add a component beyond color.

In the location page above, we removed the bright red color blocking for a subtle blue color. This gives a nice transition while adding fullness to the page. In the navigation bar, location is highlighted red, letting the user know where they are. The directions button is in the logo’s red shade to draw the users eye to it.
